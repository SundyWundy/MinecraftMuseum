First Floor Blocks
Block Name Block Texture Description
Sandstone Slab Sandstone slabs are crafted by placing 3 sandstone blocks in a vertical line in the crafting table. They are primarily used as a decorative block.
Sand Sand generates naturally in many biomes of the Overworld near lakes or ponds, notably deserts, beaches, and rivers, generally in four-block-deep layers.
Smooth Sandstone Smooth sandstone has been made obtainable by smelting regular sandstone, which can be used to craft smooth sandstone slabs and stairs.
Cut Sandstone Cut sandstone can also be crafted, by placing four blocks of Sandstone in a 2x2 crafting grid
Chiseled Sandstone Chiseled sandstone is unaffected by gravity, and it will not fall if it's placed without a supporting block underneath it, much like the other types of sandstone.
Grass Block Grass blocks were the second block introduced in Minecraft, after cobblestone. When first introduced, the grass block had its top side texture on all sides.
Dirt Block Dirt is the most common subsurface block in the Overworld, making up the majority of the top layers of blocks, between grass, mycelium, snow, and stone.
Oak Block In Minecraft, oak wood with bark is an oak wood block that is fully covered in bark and is one of the many building blocks that you can make.
Birch Log Iron ore is among the many kinds of ore that can be harvested to make tools or armor. Has decent durability and is commonly found.
Spruce Log Gold ore can be rarer and hard to find but can produce even higher quality items.
Birch Planks Birch Wood is used to make Birch Planks, which are important Crafting ingredients. It can also be cooked in a Furnace to obtain Charcoal.
Oak Planks In Minecraft, wood planks including oak wood planks are another important item in your inventory that are used to make many other items in the game. Oak planks can also be used as a fuel in a furnace to smelt/cook 1.5 items
Podzol Podzol replaces mycelium, grass blocks, dirt and coarse dirt around a large spruce tree when it is grown from 4 saplings.
Coarse Dirt Coarse Dirt is a variation of dirt found in mega taiga, mesa, and savanna biomes that neither grass , Podzol or Mycelium will grow on top of despite the light level
Poppy Poppies, formerly named Roses, are flowers in Minecraft. They are a bit taller than the Dandelion. Poppies can be grown on Grass Blocks
Dandelion Dandelions, also known as Flowers or Yellow Flowers, are most certainly the most common flower in Minecraft. They can be found in any grassy biome.
Spruce Sapling Spruce Saplings grow into Spruce Trees if they have at least 7 Blocks of Air above them and enough Sunlight.
Kelp Kelp can be planted on a broad variety of blocks. It grows underwater if it has either a source block of water or flowing water above it.
Sea Pickle Sea pickles produce light when underwater. A single pickle produces a light level of 6, and a colony produces an additional 3 levels per pickle
Seagrass Seagrass is a non-solid plant block that generates in all oceans, except frozen oceans. Seagrass generates in either its tall or small form in ocean biomes near kelp, and also generates by itself in rivers, underwater caves and swamps, replacing ice
Lily Pad A lily pad is a short, flat non-solid block that can be found naturally growing only on water, in swamps and wheat farm rooms in woodland mansions.
Packed Ice Packed ice is an opaque solid block variant of ice. Unlike regular ice, it does not melt under contact with the sun.
Snow Block A snow block is a full-sized block of snow. If mined with a Silk Touch enchanted shovel, snow blocks drop themselves; without the enchantment, snowballs are dropped instead.
Acacia Log A log or stem is a naturally occurring block found in trees or huge fungi, primarily used to create planks. It comes in eight types: oak, spruce, birch, jungle, acacia, dark oak, crimson and warped.
Stripped Spruce A stripped log or stripped stem is a variant obtained by using an axe on a log or a stem respectively.
Block of RedStone A block of redstone acts as a piston-movable redstone power source that cannot be turned off.
Block of Gold A block of gold is a precious metal block equivalent to nine gold ingots.
Block of Coal A block of coal is a mineral block that can also be used as a fuel.
Obsidian Obsidian is a dark purple block known for its high blast resistance and strength, most commonly found when water flows over lava.
Block of Diamond A block of diamond is a precious mineral block. Diamond blocks spawn as the cores of "obsidian rooms" and "lava rooms" in woodland mansions.
Block of Emerald A block of emerald is a precious mineral block. A block of emerald can also be used underneath a note block to get the "Bit" sound.
Lapis Lazuli Block A lapis lazuli block/ block of lapis lazuli‌is a decorative mineral block that is crafted from lapis lazuli. It is a medium blue color, with darker and lighter blue markings on it.
Block of Iron A block of iron is a precious metal block equivalent to nine iron ingots. It can be used to make a pyramid for a beacon, spawn iron golems, and for other things such as building.
Jungle Sapling A sapling is a non-solid block that can be grown into a tree. Jungle needs at least 5 spaces above (3×3 column) when placing a single sapling.
Jungle Log A log or stem is a naturally occurring block found in trees or huge fungi, primarily used to create planks. It comes in eight types: oak, spruce, birch, jungle, acacia, dark oak, crimson and warped.
Jungle Leaves Leaves are blocks that grow as part of trees. Leaves mainly drop saplings and sticks. They may also drop apples (oak and dark oak trees only).
Stripped Jungle Log A stripped log or stripped stem is a variant obtained by using an axe on a log or a stem respectively. Can be made using an axe on a log.
Jungle Stairs Stairs are blocks that allow mobs and players to change elevation without jumping. These are made with jungle wood.
Jungle Planks Jungle planks are generated as part of Shipwrecks.
Netherrack Netherrack is a rock-like block found in the Nether.
Soul Soil Soul soil is a block naturally found only in the soul sand valley.
Soul Sand Soul sand is a block found naturally only in the Nether. Soul sand usually slows down the movement of mobs and players standing on it. It is also used for growing nether wart and for constructing a Wither.
Mycelium Mycelium is a variant of dirt that is found naturally in mushroom fields biomes. It has a particle effect that resembles tiny spores being released constantly from the surface.
Nether Brick A nether brick is an item made by smelting netherrack in a furnace.
Nether Quartz Ore Nether quartz ore is ore found in the Nether, and is a source of quartz.
Crimson Fungus A fungus is a mushroom-like block that generates in the Nether. There are two types of fungi: crimson and warped.
Warped Fungus A fungus is a mushroom-like block that generates in the Nether. There are two types of fungi: crimson and warped.
Endstone Brick Stairs Stairs are blocks that allow mobs and players to change elevation without jumping. Crafted with endstone.
Endstone Brick Slab Slabs are half-height versions of their respective blocks. Crafted with endstone.
Endstone Bricks End stone bricks are an end stone-based brick.
Endstone End stone is a block that appears in The End and, apart from generated structures, makes up all of the solid ground that exists in that dimension.
Endrod An end rod is a decorative light source that emits white particles. End rods can be obtained by any tool or block.
Ender Chest Ender chests are a type of chest that can store items, and whose contents can be accessed from anywhere in the world.
End Portal Frame An end portal frame[a] is a block that resembles end stone with a decorative bluish-green top, with sides decorated in hollow rounded rectangles with the same color as the top.
Crying Obsidian Crying obsidian is a luminous variant of obsidian that can be used to craft a respawn anchor and produces purple particles when placed.
Smooth Quartz Block Crafted from a block of quartz, a smooth quartz block is a quartz block with no groovez or lines, just a smooth block.
Chiseled Quartz Block Crafted from a block of quartz, a chiseled quartz block is essentially a quartz block with a different style.
Block of Quartz A block of quartz is a mineral block used only for decoration. It can be turned into a chiseled quartz block, quartz pillar, smooth quartz block, or quartz bricks.
Quartz Pillar The pillar quartz block is almost exactly the same as the quartz block. The only difference is the texture.
Purpur Block Purpur blocks and purpur pillars are decorative blocks that are naturally generated in end city dungeons and end ships in the End.
Purpur Pillar Purpur Pillars are one of the main material blocks that End Cities and End Ships are constructed from.
Purpur Stairs Purpur stairs generate naturally in end cities. Oak, dark oak and birch stairs as well as cobblestone stairs generate naturally in woodland mansions.
Purpur Slab Purpur slabs are crafted by placing 3 purpur blocks in a vertical line in the crafting table. They are primarily used as a decorative block.
